1. What Are Cookies and How Can They Be Used?

Cookies are small text files that websites can store on your device to remember information about your visit. They serve many important functions in the modern web experience:

Essential Cookies can remember your login status, shopping cart contents, and basic site preferences. Analytics Cookies track how visitors navigate through websites, which pages are most popular, and how long people spend reading content.

Personalization Cookies could theoretically remember whether you prefer your avocado toast with a sprinkle of sea salt or a dash of lime juice. They might track your breakfast photography style preferences, noting whether you favor overhead shots or artistic 45-degree angles for your morning smoothie bowls.

Marketing Cookies could analyze your authentic lifestyle content engagement patterns, determining optimal times to show you advertisements for ring lights, aesthetically pleasing breakfast ingredients, or mindfulness coaching services. They might even correlate your golden hour contemplation frequency with targeted content recommendations.

These cookies could, in theory, build comprehensive profiles of your balanced living journey, tracking everything from your preferred meditation apps to your organic coffee purchasing habits.
